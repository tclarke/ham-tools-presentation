Useful Tools for the HAM Maker

Abstract

This presentation is an introduction to some software tools that are useful for design, build, and repair of radio related projects.

In order to make the presentation more relevant, we'll be designing a relatively simple but useful circuit, a microphone preamp and vocal compressor.

While this will be focused on the tools, I will go over some basic theory so that everyone can follow along.

High level design

  • Electret: A condenser mic that self-charges and has a build-in JFET
  • LNA: Must work over vocal frequency range.
  • LPF: remove harmonics from the amplifier
  • Compressor: Compress the dynamic range so you get a more consistent SSB power level.

Microphone

  • Electret has a permanent static charge
  • Needs to be biased
  • Very low output level
    • It has a JFET amp built-in
  • We still need to set the drive reactance and AC couple

Low Noise Amplifier and Filter

  • OPAMPs are great for audio frequencies
  • Boost the gain more than needed and include a passive LPF
  • Or just use an active filter with gain

Exercise - Design a LPF with Gain

  • Analog Devices Filter Wizard
  • Transfer design to KiCad

Audio Compressor

  • Speech has lots of variation volume (50 dB)
    • Can be difficult to hear soft sounds over noise
    • Highest volume will maximally drive SSB
    • Large dynamic range means lower average power!
  • Compresses the dynamic range by change amount of volume reduction

A Note on Qucs

  • Very complete simulation including digital and mixed-mode
  • Not a lot of models
  • Steep learning curve
  • The only way to go from VHF, UHF, and microwave
